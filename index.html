Delivery Model

A process built to de-risk architecture before scale increases cost.

The best results come from exposing hardware, connectivity, platform, and user-flow decisions early. The engagement model is designed to make those tradeoffs explicit before they become release friction.

01

Discover

Define the device behavior, environment, data path, integration surfaces, and the operational decisions the product must support.

02

Prototype

De-risk the radio layer, gateway path, provisioning assumptions, and the primary workflow before the build hardens.

03

Build

Implement firmware, services, dashboards, and apps with observability, release structure, and supportability built in.

04

Deploy

Prepare the system for rollout with update paths, field feedback loops, issue visibility, and operational refinement.

Operating Model

What a healthy engagement usually needs.

Teams typically bring Causeve in when the product spans hardware constraints, field connectivity, and downstream application behavior that cannot be solved in isolation.

  • Architecture review before implementation reduces complexity
  • Provisioning, OTA, diagnostics, and fleet support accounted for early
  • Clear contracts between firmware, gateway, backend, and app surfaces
  • Leadership-level involvement through the technical decisions that matter
Company Approach

Connected systems with more engineering continuity.

Since 2021, Causeve has focused on building connected products across embedded software, wireless systems, Linux gateways, cloud services, and operator applications. The emphasis is practical: build systems that can be deployed, updated, monitored, and operated with confidence.

Founded by Ilango Thavamani and Parthiban Balaraman, the company brings 22+ years of combined experience across automotive embedded software, IoT devices, electronics, and connected product delivery.

We keep device, gateway, backend, and application decisions aligned from prototype through rollout so the product behaves as one system instead of a collection of disconnected layers.

Reliability before novelty

Decisions are judged against field behavior, not only prototype speed or interface polish.

System thinking

Device, gateway, backend, and operator workflows are designed as one coordinated system for the product.

Clear release paths

Provisioning, updates, diagnostics, and observability are part of delivery instead of deferred cleanup.

Hands-on leadership

Senior technical context stays close to the work through discovery, build, validation, and rollout.
